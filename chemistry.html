Chemicals Found in Cigarettes

  • Carbon Monoxide (CO): Toxic byproduct of industrial processes
  • Lead (Pb): Extremely toxic heavy metal used in batteries and construction sites
  • Nicotine (C10H14N2): Addictive substance also used in insecticides
  • Formaldehyde (CH2O): Common disinfectant and preservative
  • Acetone (C3H6O): Found in nail polish removers and cleaning products
  • Hydrogen Cyanide (HCN): Highly toxic byproduct in the production of plastics
  • Benzene (C6H6): Carcinogenic compound
  • Arsenic (As): Highly poisonous chemical
  • Ammonia (NH3): Used as a common fertilizer
  • Tar: Complex mixture of substances found in cigarette smoke, contributes to various respiratory diseases

Treatment of Some Harmful Compounds

  • H2O + CO → CO2 + H2, gets rid of carbon monoxide
  • AsCl3 + 3H2 → As + 3HCl, reduction reaction that creates elemental arsenic, which is much less toxic than arsenic compounds
  • HCN + H2O2 → N2 + H2O, oxidizes hydrogen cyanide to less toxic compounds
